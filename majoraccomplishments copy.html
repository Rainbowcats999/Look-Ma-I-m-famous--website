Major accomplishments: As President, he built the Republican Party into a strong national organization. Further, he rallied most of the northern Democrats to the Union cause. On January 1, 1863, he issued the Emancipation Proclamation that declared forever free those slaves within the Confederacy.

Preserving the Union:

Lincoln's primary goal during the Civil War was to keep the United States together, which he successfully achieved by leading the Union to victory against the Confederacy.

Emancipation Proclamation:

This executive order, issued on January 1, 1863, declared freedom for enslaved people in Confederate territories, significantly turning the tide of the war by allowing African Americans to join the Union army.

13th Amendment:

Though not fully enacted during Lincoln's presidency, he strongly advocated for the 13th Amendment which ultimately abolished slavery nationwide.
